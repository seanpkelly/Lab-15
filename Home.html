So my name is Sean Kelly, and this little website is about me and work I do, and the work I've done.

As a poet, and author of fiction I've had work featured in:

The Detroit Free Press

The Paris Review

The Macomb Daily

The Western Herald

Just to name a few publications.

My style is free form poetry that reflects personal experience of loss.

Yes, all of my poetry is either about love or death. That's the way it goes sometimes.


I got into Web Development by having friends who had gotten into the field and falling in love with it.

They were constantly telling me that it was a field where I would be appreciated for my intellect.

I myself had been looking to transition into a field where I was both compensated more appropriately,

and able to grow more easily towards my goals. Web Development and the entire world of tech allows that.

As I work towards those goals I can pictrue myself in a career where I have insurance, benefits, paid time off.

The reason I'm putting every ounce of me into the Grand Circus bootcamp is not merely a pride thing.

It's just as much to do with the fact that I really want to be a part of this industry, debugging and

writing code for a living. That feeling makes all of this worth it.


Where I come from is as important to me as the people I grew up with.

I was born in Sarasota, Florida and immediately my parents moved us to Denver, Colorado where they first met and lived before they moved to Florida.

After a short amount of time in Colorado my parents moved us to Detroit, Michigan where my father was born and raised.

From there I spent my life living in seven different homes from the city to the a 60 acre horse farm and back to the city.

My life has always been in flux, and most of it spent being raised by my incredible single mother who would do things I still

can't fathom myself being able to do.

Simply put, my Mother is one amazing woman. With that being said, my hometown as it was, is Saint Clair Shores, Michigan.

Saint Clair Shores is where I went to high school, it's where I spent the most amount of time during my youth and it's where my Grandparents live.
